## Battle Card 1: ServiceNow GRC

### Overview
**Company:** ServiceNow (NYSE: NOW)  
**Product:** ServiceNow GRC (Governance, Risk, and Compliance)  
**Market Position:** Market leader in enterprise GRC  
**Malaysia Presence:** Strong (partners: PwC, Strateq)

### Strengths
- ✅ Brand recognition and enterprise credibility
- ✅ Integrated platform (ITSM + GRC + Security)
- ✅ Strong partner ecosystem in Malaysia
- ✅ Proven implementation methodology
- ✅ Case study: AEON Bank (June 2025 go-live)

### Weaknesses
- ❌ **June 2026 Security Incident:** ServiceNow platform exploited (publicly reported)
- ❌ Generic GRC module (not RMiT-native)
- ❌ High TCO (RM 1.5M-2.5M over 3 years)
- ❌ Long implementation (6-12 months typical)
- ❌ Complex customization required for RMiT
- ❌ US-based support (time zone challenges)

### Pricing Intelligence
- **License:** RM 500K-800K/year (depending on modules)
- **Implementation:** RM 500K-1M (one-time)
- **Maintenance:** 20% of license fee annually
- **Total 3-Year TCO:** RM 2.5M-4M

### Positioning Strategy
**Against ServiceNow:**
- "ServiceNow is a great platform for ITSM, but their GRC module is generic. RMiT requires specific controls, evidence types, and Board reporting that ServiceNow doesn't provide out-of-the-box. After their June 2026 security incident, [X] Malaysian FIs are re-evaluating their GRC strategy."

**Kill Questions:**
1. "How will you customize ServiceNow's generic GRC for RMiT's 8 domains?"
2. "What's your plan B if ServiceNow implementation runs over 12 months?"
3. "How does ServiceNow's June 2026 incident affect your risk assessment?"
4. "Can ServiceNow provide BNM-ready reports without customization?"

### Objection Handling
**"We're already using ServiceNow for ITSM."**
→ "That's great! Many of our customers started with ServiceNow. The question is: does your current ServiceNow instance meet RMiT requirements? Most need significant customization. We can integrate with your existing ServiceNow deployment for ITSM while providing RMiT-native GRC capabilities."

**"ServiceNow is the safe choice."**
→ "ServiceNow is certainly well-known. However, after their June 2026 security incident, 'safe' is relative. Plus, BNM doesn't mandate any specific vendor — they mandate compliance outcomes. Our platform is purpose-built for RMiT, with 80% of evidence collection automated out-of-the-box."

## Battle Card 7: Build vs. Buy

### Overview
**Approach:** Build custom GRC platform in-house  
**Typical Proponent:** CTO/CIO with development team  
**Decision Driver:** "We can build it cheaper ourselves"

### Strengths (Perceived)
- ✅ Full control over features and roadmap
- ✅ No vendor lock-in
- ✅ Can customize exactly to requirements
- ✅ Uses internal development resources

### Weaknesses (Reality)
- ❌ **3-Year TCO:** RM 3.95M+ (vs. RM 2.85M buy)
- ❌ **Timeline:** 12-18 months (vs. 6-8 weeks buy)
- ❌ **Team:** 5-8 FTEs required (dev, QA, security, compliance SME)
- ❌ **Maintenance:** Ongoing updates, security patches, regulatory changes
- ❌ **Validation:** BNM may question custom-built platform validity
- ❌ **Opportunity Cost:** Dev team not working on revenue-generating projects

### Build Cost Breakdown (3 Years)
| Cost Component | Amount (RM) |
|----------------|-------------|
| Development Team (5 FTEs × RM 150K × 3 years) | 2,250,000 |
| Infrastructure (servers, databases, tools) | 500,000 |
| Security Audits (annual) | 300,000 |
| External Penetration Testing (annual) | 150,000 |
| Compliance SME Consultation | 200,000 |
| Ongoing Maintenance (Year 2-3) | 500,000 |
| **Total** | **3,900,000+** |

### Buy Cost Breakdown (3 Years)
| Cost Component | Amount (RM) |
|----------------|-------------|
| Platform License (RM 950K × 3 years) | 2,850,000 |
| Implementation | Included |
| Training | Included |
| Support & Updates | Included |
| **Total** | **2,850,000** |

### Savings: **RM 1.05M+ over 3 years**

### Positioning Strategy
**Against Build:**
- "Building in-house seems cheaper initially, but the math tells a different story. Over 3 years, building costs RM 3.9M+ vs. RM 2.85M for buying. Plus, you're live in 6 weeks vs. 12-18 months. And BNM has validated our platform — can they say the same for a custom build?"

**Kill Questions:**
1. "Can your development team deliver a production-ready GRC platform in 6 weeks?"
2. "How will BNM validate your custom-built platform during their examination?"
3. "What happens when your lead GRC developer leaves the company?"
4. "Who will maintain the platform and implement regulatory updates when RMiT changes?"
5. "Have you calculated the opportunity cost of 5 developers working on GRC vs. revenue-generating projects?"

### Objection Handling
**"We have capable developers in-house."**
→ "I'm sure you do. But GRC development requires specific expertise: RMiT regulatory knowledge, evidence automation, Board reporting, BNM integration. This isn't typical CRUD application development. Plus, can your developers start tomorrow, or are they already committed to other projects?"

**"We want full control over the roadmap."**
→ "That's understandable. However, RMiT is a regulatory requirement, not a competitive differentiator. Your roadmap should focus on customer-facing innovations, not compliance infrastructure. We handle the compliance roadmap so you can focus on your core business."

**"Building is more cost-effective long-term."**
→ "Let's do the math together. [Walk through TCO analysis]. Over 3 years, buying saves RM 1M+. Over 5 years, the savings increase to RM 2M+ because we continue to invest in platform improvements while your build costs compound."

## Usage Guidelines

### When to Use Battle Cards:
1. **Discovery Call:** Prospect mentions evaluating competitors
2. **Proposal Stage:** Include differentiation section
3. **POC Execution:** Highlight your strengths during POC
4. **Objection Handling:** Prospect raises competitor comparison
5. **Executive Briefing:** Board-level competitive landscape

### Best Practices:
- ✅ **Verify Information:** Competitive intel changes rapidly
- ✅ **Stay Professional:** Don't disparage competitors; focus on facts
- ✅ **Use Customer Stories:** "Customer X migrated from ServiceNow because..."
- ✅ **Quantify Differences:** TCO, timeline, resource requirements
- ✅ **Acknowledge Strengths:** Credibility requires balanced view

### Red Flags (Avoid):
- ❌ Don't make unverified claims
- ❌ Don't use confidential information
- ❌ Don't focus only on negatives (acknowledge competitor strengths)
- ❌ Don't overpromise on your capabilities
