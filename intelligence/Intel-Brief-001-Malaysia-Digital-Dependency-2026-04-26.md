## 3. Intelligence Content

### 3.1 Digital Dependency Statistics (Malaysia)

| Metric | Value | Date | Implication |
|--------|-------|------|-------------|
| **Internet Users** | 35.4 million | End 2025 | ~98% of population digitally connected |
| **Internet Penetration** | 98% | End 2025 | Internet = basic necessity, not luxury |
| **Mobile Access** | 97.9% of users | End 2025 | Smartphone = primary digital device |
| **Mobile Connection Rate** | 121.9% | End 2025 | Avg person has >1 SIM/connection |
| **Monthly Data Usage** | ~25GB per user | End 2025 | High bandwidth consumption (streaming, social, e-commerce) |

**Strategic Assessment:**

> "Internet connectivity and networks are strategic and critical assets for any country... A large-scale internet blackout would not be impossible to cause panic."
>
> — Siraj Jalil, President MCCA

**Operational Implication:** Any disruption to digital infrastructure has **national security implications**, not just technical inconveniences. This elevates cybersecurity from IT issue to **national defense priority**.

---

### 3.2 National Cybersecurity Architecture

#### 3.2.1 Legal Framework

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Legislation** | Cybersecurity Act 2024 (Act 854) |
| **Status** | Enforced (2026) |
| **Lead Agency** | NACSA (National Cyber Security Agency) |
| **Mandate** | Coordinate prevention, monitoring, response to cyber threats |

#### 3.2.2 Operational Structure

| Component | Function | Status |
|-----------|----------|--------|
| **NC4** | National Cyber Coordination and Control Center | **Operational** |
| **Role** | 24/7 monitoring of National Critical Information Infrastructure (NCII) | Active |
| **Capabilities** | Real-time threat monitoring, coordinated response | Deployed |
| **Supporting Agencies** | CyberSecurity Malaysia (CSM), CSIRT | Integrated |

#### 3.2.3 National Critical Information Infrastructure (NCII)

| Sector | Classification | Protection Level |
|--------|----------------|------------------|
| **Energy** | NCII | Highest |
| **Water** | NCII | Highest |
| **Finance** | NCII | Highest |
| **Telecommunications** | NCII | Highest |
| **Transportation** | NCII | Highest |
| **Government** | NCII | Highest |

**Strategic Assessment:**

> "Through integrated approach, NC4 enables faster and more effective response actions, reducing impact of attacks on national operations."
>
> — Ir Dr Megat Zuhairy Megat Tajuddin, CEO NACSA

**Operational Implication:** NC4 is the **central node** for all cyber threat intelligence. Any sovereign intelligence platform must integrate with or complement NC4's capabilities.

---

### 3.3 National Cyber Capabilities

| Capability | Agency | Maturity |
|------------|--------|----------|
| **Penetration Testing** | NACSA, CSM, CSIRT | Established |
| **Threat Intelligence** | NACSA, NC4 | Growing |
| **Incident Analysis** | NACSA, CSM | Established |
| **Digital Forensics** | NACSA, CSM | Established |
| **Real-time Monitoring** | NC4 | Operational |

**Strategic Assessment:**

> "The country is no longer in a phase of just defending against cyber threats, but is moving aggressively to strengthen national digital resilience comprehensively."
>
> — Ir Dr Megat Zuhairy Megat Tajuddin, CEO NACSA

**Operational Implication:** Malaysia has moved from **reactive defense** to **proactive resilience**. This creates demand for:
- Predictive threat analytics
- Multi-source intelligence correlation
- Automated threat detection
- Sovereign deployment (no foreign cloud dependency)
