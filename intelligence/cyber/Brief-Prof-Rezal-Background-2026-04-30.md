### What is CBOM?

**Cyber Bill of Materials (CBOM)** = Structured inventory of cybersecurity assets, dependencies, and controls within an organization's supply chain.

### CBOM vs. SBOM

| SBOM (Software Bill of Materials) | CBOM (Cyber Bill of Materials) |
|-----------------------------------|--------------------------------|
| Software components only | All cyber assets (hardware, software, services) |
| Development-focused | Operations + Supply Chain-focused |
| Vendor-driven | Operator-driven |
| Example: CycloneDX, SPDX | Example: Emerging (NIST, CISA drafts) |

### CBOM Use Cases

1. **Supply Chain Visibility** — Know what you have, where it came from
2. **Vulnerability Response** — Rapid impact analysis (e.g., Log4j-style incidents)
3. **Compliance Reporting** — Regulatory requirements (BNM SC, NACSA directives)
4. **Risk Quantification** — DRQ (Digital Risk Quantification) input data
5. **Incident Response** — Asset context for forensics + containment

- **Automated Discovery** — Browser-Harness + AIL Framework ingestion
- **Anomaly Detection** — Scikit-learn (94% accuracy) for budget/procurement anomalies
