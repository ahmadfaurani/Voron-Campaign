## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

**N.14 Bukit Naning** is a **4-cornered contest** between Barisan Nasional (BN), Pakatan Harapan (PH), Perikatan Nasional (PN), and Parti Bersama Rakyat (Bersama). This seat is a **critical battleground** in the Muar/Bakri area, with a history of swing voting between BN and PH.

### Key Facts at a Glance

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **Total Registered Voters (2026)** | 21,992 |
| **Ethnic Composition** | 66.5% Malay, 35.8% Chinese, 1.7% Others |
| **2022 Result** | BN (Mohd Fuad Tukirin) won with 5,437 votes (43.0%), majority 1,535 |
| **2018 Result** | PH (Md Ysahrudin Kusni) won with 7,280 votes (48.4%), majority 1,552 |
| **Turnout 2022** | 64.4% |
| **Turnout 2018** | 86.0% |
| **2026 Contest** | 4-cornered (BN vs PH vs PN vs Bersama) |

### The Bottom Line

Bukit Naning is a **toss-up seat** highly sensitive to turnout and vote-splitting dynamics. The entry of **Bersama (Iskandar Md Alias)** as a fourth candidate introduces significant uncertainty—potentially splitting the Malay vote and altering the outcome. PH's Md Ysahrudin must maximize Chinese voter support (35.8%) while retaining enough Malay support to overcome BN's incumbent advantage. BN's strategy hinges on consolidating Malay votes and maintaining the 2022 coalition with PAS tacit support.

**Main Risks:**
1. **Bersama vote split** — Could draw 5-10% from PH or BN, depending on candidate profile
2. **Low turnout scenario** — Favors BN's machinery; PH needs 80%+ turnout to win
3. **PN spoiler effect** — Mohd Radzi Amin (PN) could draw 15-20% from BN's traditional base

**What Needs Confirming:**
- Bersama candidate's exact positioning and ground machinery
- Early voting patterns (military/police: ~912 voters)
- Final turnout projections by polling district
