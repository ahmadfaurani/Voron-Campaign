## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The 16th Johor State Election (PRN Johor) concluded successfully on **11 July 2026** with BN winning 48 of 56 seats and PH winning 8. PDRM deployed 14,000+ personnel and reported security "under control" throughout. 90 police reports and 25 investigation papers were filed during the campaign — none involving candidates. One phantom voter allegation emerged (Senggarang). PDRM has now **pivoted to Negeri Sembilan** (polling 1 August), with IGP visiting the contingent on 15 July and riot simulations completed. Domestically, Deputy IGP Ayob Khan pushed "Smart Policing" via AI/digitalization at PDRM Innovation Day (16 July). Home Minister Saifuddin Nasution confirmed Interpol Red Notice for fugitive activist Tamim Dahri and disclosed 33 Sedition Act prosecutions.

### 2.4 ⚠️ PHANTOM VOTER ALLEGATION — SENGGARANG (11 Jul 2026)
- **Source:** Malaysiakini, Sinar Daily, The Star
- **Allegant:** Onn Abu Bakar — PH candidate for Senggarang state seat (Batu Pahat)
- **Incident:** A 25-year-old male voter was unable to cast his ballot at SMK Senggarang because his name had allegedly already been used by another individual
- **Time detected:** ~10:30am
- **Action:** Police report lodged at Senggarang Police Station ~11am
- **Senggarang contest:** Three-cornered — Onn Abu Bakar (PH) vs Mohd Yusla Ismail (BN, incumbent) vs Datuk Mohd Rashid Hasnon (PN-Bersatu)
- **Note:** Two PH candidates raised phantom voter concerns; voters complained of inability to cast ballots
- **Status:** Under investigation; EC urged to ensure all eligible voters can exercise rights

### 2.5 Election Result
- **BN: 48 seats | PH: 8 seats** (BN retained two-thirds supermajority)
- No untoward incidents reported on polling day
