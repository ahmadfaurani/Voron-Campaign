### Key News Beat Findings

#### A. IGP Press Conference on Polling Day (11 July)
- **Sources:** Malay Mail, The Star, Bernama, NST, Scoop, FMT, Harian Metro, BHarian
- **90 police reports** received during 14-day campaign period
- **25 investigation papers** opened
- **Zero candidates or senior party leaders** implicated in any reports
- Most reports: "acts of mischief, including the damaging, removal and burning of political party flags"
- All investigation papers "still being processed to identify those responsible"
- Voting process proceeded "smoothly with no untoward incidents reported"
- Polling continued until 6 PM on 11 July

**Legal Framework for Investigations:**
- Penal Code (Sections 427, 435, 500)
- Communications and Multimedia Act 1998 (Section 233)
- Election Offences Act 1954 (Section 4A(1))

#### B. Campaign Permit Statistics
- **Total applications (27 Jun – 8 Jul):** 4,368
- **Approved:** 4,053 (~92.8%)
- **July 7-8 applications:** 884 → 838 approved

#### C. Deployment Progression
- **18 June:** 11,926 personnel announced (5 phases)
- **6 July:** 3,565 personnel for early voting (647 officers, 2,806 rank-and-file, 112 civilian staff)
- **11 July:** ~14,000 total (including 5,600 from other contingents)

#### D. ⚠️ PHANTOM VOTER ALLEGATION — Senggarang (11 July)
- **Source:** NST, The Star, FMT, Newswav (Bernama)
- **PH Candidate:** Onn Abu Bakar (Senggarang state seat, Batu Pahat)
- **Location:** SMK Senggarang polling centre
- **Time:** ~10:30 AM, 11 July 2026
- **Incident:** 25-year-old male voter unable to cast ballot — someone else allegedly voted using his name
- **Police Report:** Lodged at Senggarang Police Station at ~11:00 AM
- **Candidate Statement (Facebook video):** "I urge the public to vote as early as possible and ensure that your vote is secure and has not been used by someone else."
- **EC Response:** None at press time
- **Contest Details:** 3-cornered fight — Onn (PH) vs. Mohd Yusla Ismail (BN-incumbent) vs. Datuk Mohd Rashid Hasnon (PN-Bersatu)
- **Relevance:** Meets ALERT CRITERIA — phantom voter report with police report filed

#### E. Early Voting (7 July)
- Early voting proceeded smoothly statewide
- No untoward incidents at police/armed forces polling centres
- Heavy rain at IPK Johor (~9:40 AM) did not disrupt voting
- Injured officer Sgt Syahrizal Musa (Achilles tendon, wheelchair) cast early vote despite medical leave

#### F. Post-Election (12-14 July)
- **BN victory:** 48 seats (UMNO 36, MCA 8, MIC 4) — best since 2008
- **PH:** 8 seats (DAP 6, AMANAH 1, PKR 1)
- **PN:** 0 seats — complete wipeout
- **Turnout:** 69.57% (up 14.65pp from previous)
- **55 candidates forfeited deposits** (RM275,000 to EC)
- No post-election security incidents reported in monitored sources
- BN leader Zambry: victory "reflects mandate for a stable, capable state govt"
- Political analysis: Result seen as "warning for PH," pressure on Anwar for early GE
