## 2. ELECTION SECURITY — JOHOR PRN ke-16

### 2.1 POLLING DAY STATUS (11 July 2026)
- **Outcome:** POLLING COMPLETED SMOOTHLY — NO MAJOR INCIDENTS
- **IGP assessment:** "We have not encountered any major issues. Overall, everything has been running well and smoothly, and we will continue monitoring the situation throughout polling day to ensure the process remains peaceful and orderly." — IGP Tan Sri Mohd Khalid Ismail
- **Polling centres visited by IGP:** SMK Taman Molek (13 polling streams, >8,000 registered voters) in DUN Johor Jaya
- **Johor Police Chief accompanying IGP:** Datuk Ab Rahaman Arsad
- **Voting hours:** Until 6:00 PM, 11 July 2026
- **Total registered voters:** 2.7 million+
- **Result:** BN won landslide victory. Datuk Onn Hafiz Ghazi sworn in as Johor MB on 12 July 2026.
- **Post-election:** Johor declared special state holiday on 13 July 2026.

### 2.3 NATURE OF ELECTION OFFENCES
Most cases involved **minor offences**:
- Vandalism and damage to campaign materials
- Removal, destruction, and burning of political party flags
- Acts of mischief (Section 427 Penal Code)
- Alleged promotion of ill will/hostility (Section 4A(1) Election Offences Act 1954)
- Defamation and improper use of network facilities (Section 500 Penal Code + Section 233 CMA 1998)

**IGP quote:** "Most of these are minor cases that I believe will not create uneasiness or tension among voters or election workers."
**Status:** "At this stage, all the investigation papers are still being processed to identify those responsible."
