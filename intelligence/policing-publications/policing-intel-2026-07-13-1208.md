## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The 16th Johor State Election (PRN Johor ke-16) concluded peacefully on July 11, 2026, with BN securing a landslide 48/56 seats (59.66% popular vote). PDRM deployed ~14,000 personnel statewide (>5,600 reinforcements from nationwide contingents). 90 police reports were lodged during the 14-day campaign with 25 investigation papers opened — all minor offences (vandalism, flag damage), NO candidates or senior party leaders implicated. The KPN conducted field visits on polling day and confirmed security was "terkawal" (under control). TODAY (July 13), the KPN attended the KDN Post-Cabinet Meeting Bil 19/2026 at Bukit Aman — indicating ongoing post-election security coordination. SPR focus is now shifting to the Negeri Sembilan state election.

- **Nature of offences:** Predominantly minor — vandalism, removal, destruction, and burning of political party flags; acts of mischief

- **Security assessment:** "Remained under control" throughout campaign period

### 2.4 Early Voting (Jul 7, 2026)

- **Early voting centers:** 64 total (53 PDRM, 11 ATM)

### 2.5 Overall Deployment
- **Total polling day deployment:** ~14,000 police officers and personnel
- **Reinforcements from nationwide contingents:** >5,600

- **Turnout:** 69.57% (1,897,892 votes cast; valid 1,874,918; invalid 22,974)
- **Polling ran until:** 6:00 PM, July 11, 2026

### 2.6 Election Result (for security context)
- **BN (winner):** 48/56 seats, 59.66% popular vote (+16.55pp swing) — supermajority retained, best since 2008
- **PH:** 8/56 seats, 32.64% (+6.22pp)
- **PN:** 0/56 seats, 5.45% (–19.93pp) — wiped out
- **Menteri Besar:** Onn Hafiz Ghazi (BN-UMNO) — retained
- **11 seats flipped** (mostly PH→BN)
- **55 candidates lost deposits**
- **No post-election unrest reported**
