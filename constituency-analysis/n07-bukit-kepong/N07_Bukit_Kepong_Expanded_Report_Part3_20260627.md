## **7.6 Contingency Planning — District-Level Scenarios**

### **7.6.1 Scenario: FELDA Swing to BN**

**Trigger:** Federal debt relief announcement (RM500M+) before 5 July

**Impact:**
- Tier 1 FELDA districts (A02, A03, A04, A27): BN +10-15%
- Overall BN vote share: +5-7%
- Margin shift: BN gains 1,500-2,000 votes

**Response (PN):**
- Emergency FELDA town halls (Sahruddin personal appeal)
- Counter-messaging: "State EXCO delivered more than federal"
- Rapid deployment of FELDA beneficiary testimonials

**Response (PH/MUDA):**
- Highlight FELDA structural problems (debt cycle, FGV mismanagement)
- Pivot to non-FELDA districts (urban, youth)

---

### **7.6.2 Scenario: PH Strong Candidate Entry**

**Trigger:** High-profile PH candidate announced (ex-ADUN, CEO) by 28 June

**Impact:**
- Urban districts (A01, A16, A17, A18, A24): PH +10-15%
- Chinese districts (A05, A11): PH +5-10% (consolidation)
- Overall PH vote share: +10-12%
- MUDA may stand down (vote transfer to PH)

**Response (BN/PN):**
- Attack PH federal record (2018-2020 failures)
- Emphasize DAP dominance (Chinese party narrative)
- Highlight PH candidate vulnerabilities (past record)

**Response (MUDA):**
- Accelerate stand-down decision (if leaning that way)
- Negotiate future alliance terms with PH
- Redirect volunteers to PH campaign

---

### **7.6.3 Scenario: Sahruddin Scandal**

**Trigger:** Corruption allegation, misconduct revelation (any time before 11 July)

**Impact:**
- All districts: PN -10-20% (depends on scandal severity)
- BN gains: +8-12% (primary beneficiary)
- PH/MUDA gain: +5-8% (protest vote)
- Margin shift: PN loses 2,500-4,000 votes

**Response (PN):**
- Immediate denial + legal threat (if false)
- If true: damage control (resignation consideration, third-party candidate)
- Pivot to "witch hunt" narrative (political motivation)

**Response (BN/PH/MUDA):**
- Amplify scandal (media, social media)
- Demand Sahruddin withdrawal
- Prepare for replacement candidate (if applicable)

---

### **7.6.4 Scenario: Low Turnout (<55%)**

**Trigger:** Poor early voting turnout; lackluster campaign events

**Impact:**
- Favors incumbent (PN) — core voters turn out
- Disproportionately hurts opposition (youth, protest voters stay home)
- Margin shift: PN +2-4%

**Response (Opposition):**
- Emergency GOTV campaign (transport, reminders)
- Social media blitz ("your vote matters")
- Community leader mobilization (religious, ethnic associations)

**Response (PN):**
- Complacency risk — maintain GOTV discipline
- Avoid overconfidence (public messaging)
