**Legal Basis:** Federal Gazette P.U. (B) 437, 18 July 2017  
**First Contested:** 14th General Election (9 May 2018)  
**Rationale:** Redelineation to address population growth in Muar District; created from parts of:

## **5.5 Electoral System Mechanics**

### **5.5.1 First-Past-the-Post (FPTP) Dynamics**

**System:** Plurality wins (no runoff required)  
**Implication:** Candidate with most votes wins, even if <50%

**2022 Example:**
- Sahruddin won with 44.27% (not majority)
- If BN + MUDA had coordinated (41.08% + 13.79% = 54.87%), BN would have won
- Vote splitting cost BN the seat

**2026 Scenarios:**

| Scenario | PN Vote | BN Vote | PH Vote | MUDA Vote | Winner |
|----------|---------|---------|---------|-----------|--------|
| **4-Cornered Fight** | 42% | 40% | 12% | 6% | PN (plurality) |
| **PH Stands Down** | 45% | 43% | 0% | 12% | PN (narrow) |
| **MUDA Stands Down (PH)** | 40% | 42% | 18% | 0% | BN (majority) |
| **MUDA Stands Down (BN)** | 43% | 45% | 10% | 2% | BN (majority) |
| **3-Cornered (No MUDA)** | 44% | 41% | 15% | 0% | PN (plurality) |

**Strategic Imperative:** Coordination (or lack thereof) among opposition determines outcome.
