### 4.2 Weekly Reporting

#### Weekly Business Review (WBR) - Every Monday 10:00 AM
**Attendees:** CEO, VP Sales, Sales Manager, SDR Lead, AE Lead  
**Duration:** 60 minutes  
**Format:** Dashboard review + blocker discussion + action items

**WBR Agenda:**
1. **Last Week's Performance (10 min)**
   - Emails Sent vs. Target
   - Responses vs. Target
   - Meetings Booked vs. Target
   - POCs Launched vs. Target
   - Opportunities Created vs. Target
   - Deals Closed vs. Target
   - Revenue vs. Target

2. **Pipeline Review (20 min)**
   - Top 10 Opportunities (by value)
   - Stalled Deals (no movement >14 days)
   - At-Risk Deals (competitor, budget, timeline)
   - POC Status (active, blocked, successful, failed)

3. **Blocker Discussion (15 min)**
   - SDR Blockers (no responses, wrong contacts, etc.)
   - AE Blockers (procurement delays, legal issues, etc.)
   - Product Blockers (POC issues, feature gaps, etc.)
   - Marketing Blockers (content needs, event support, etc.)

4. **Action Items (15 min)**
   - Assign owners to each blocker
   - Set deadlines for resolution
   - Escalate critical issues to CEO
   - Celebrate wins (deal closures, big POCs, etc.)

**WBR Output:**
- Updated forecast (committed, best case, pipeline)
- Blocker resolution plan (owner + deadline)
- Resource reallocation (if needed)
- Motivational recognition (top performers)

### 5.3 Objection Handling Playbook

#### Common Objections & Responses:

**"We're already using ServiceNow."**
→ Response: "Many of our customers started with ServiceNow. They migrated to VoronDRQ because: (1) RMiT-native vs. generic GRC, (2) 50-70% lower TCO, (3) 4-8 week implementation vs. 6-12 months. After ServiceNow's June 2026 security incident, [X] Malaysian FIs migrated to us. Would you be open to a 30-minute comparison session?"

**"We don't have budget allocated."**
→ Response: "Understood. Most of our customers initially thought the same. However, BNM's revised RMiT policy (November 2025) requires compliance within 90 days. The cost of non-compliance (BNM enforcement, personal liability) far exceeds the investment. We offer Q1 pricing at RM [X]K/year, which is [X]% lower than standard. Can we explore creative financing or phased implementation?"

**"We want to build in-house."**
→ Response: "We've seen this before. Let's do the math: 5 FTEs × RM 150K/year × 3 years = RM 2.25M (salaries only). Add infrastructure, security audits, maintenance: RM 3M-5M total. VoronDRQ: RM 1.2M-2.4M over 3 years. Plus, can you have a production-ready platform in 6 weeks? Will BNM accept a custom-built platform without third-party validation? What happens when your lead developer leaves?"

**"We need to wait until next budget cycle."**
→ Response: "I understand budget cycles. However, BNM's gap analysis deadline is February 26, 2026 — that's 90 days from policy issuance. If you wait until next budget cycle, you'll be out of compliance and exposed to enforcement action. We can lock in Q1 pricing now and defer implementation start to align with your budget cycle. Would that work?"

**"We need Board approval."**
→ Response: "Absolutely. We have a Board-ready briefing deck that covers: (1) RMiT requirements and deadlines, (2) Gap analysis results, (3) Implementation timeline and budget, (4) Peer references, (5) Risk of non-compliance. We can schedule a 30-minute Board briefing with our CEO and a RMiT subject matter expert. When is your next Board Risk Committee meeting?"

**"Your pricing is too high."**
→ Response: "I understand budget is a concern. Let's compare: ServiceNow Tier 1 pricing is RM 1.5M-2.5M over 3 years (including implementation). VoronDRQ Q1 pricing is RM 950K/year × 3 = RM 2.85M all-in. That's 40-60% savings. Additionally, we automate 80% of evidence collection, which saves [X] FTEs × RM 150K/year = RM [X]K/year in operational costs. Over 3 years, total savings is RM [X]M. Does that help justify the investment?"

**"We're evaluating multiple vendors."**
→ Response: "That's smart — you should do your diligence. Can I ask which other vendors you're evaluating? [Listen]. Great, here's how we compare: [Use battle card]. Additionally, we offer a 14-day POC so you can validate our claims before committing. Would you be open to a POC alongside your other evaluations?"

**"Send me information and I'll review."**
→ Response: "Happy to send information. I'll send over: (1) RMiT Compliance Checklist (28 pages), (2) VoronDRQ Platform Deck, (3) [Tier-specific] Case Study, (4) Q1 Incentive One-Pager. To make sure the information is relevant, can I ask: (1) What's your current RMiT compliance status? (2) When is your gap analysis submission deadline? (3) Who else is involved in the evaluation? Let's schedule a 15-minute follow-up call for [DATE/TIME] to discuss your questions. Does that work?"

**"We're not a priority for BNM enforcement."**
→ Response: "I understand you may feel that way as a [Tier X institution]. However, BNM's revised RMiT policy applies to ALL regulated institutions, not just Tier 1 banks. In Q1 2026, BNM issued enforcement actions against [X] MSBs/DFIs for non-compliance. The policy explicitly states personal liability for directors and officers (RMiT para 1.3), which applies regardless of institution size. Would you like to see the enforcement tracker?"

**"We just renewed with [Competitor]."**
→ Response: "Congratulations on the renewal. When does your contract come up for renewal again? [Listen]. Great, let's stay in touch. In the meantime, I'd like to invite you to our RMiT compliance webinar on [DATE] — no pitch, just educational content on BNM requirements. Even if you're happy with [Competitor], it's good to stay informed. Would you like me to reserve a seat?"
